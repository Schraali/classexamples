Overfishing

The overharvesting and fishing of Chinook salmon impacts their population and individual sizes dramatically.

This is especially evident when faced with these two statistics and the many others like them.

  • According to a 2012 study by the Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife, commercial sport and tribal fishing reduces the amount of four and five-year-old Chinook salmon by about 20%.
  • From 1975 to 2010, almost 20 million Chinook have been harvested for commercial, sport or subsistence and ceremonial fisheries.

Fish Hatcheries

More info on fish hatcheries
Fish Hatchery Definition
A place for artificial breeding, hatching, and rearing through the early life stages of different aquatic life

Fish hatcheries damage Salmon in a several ways:


  1. Millions of salmon with genetic similarities are released into the oceans from these hatcheries
    • This will make Chinook salmon less able to survive environment changes
    • This can also cause increased competition between wild and hatchery stocks
  2. Most fish leaving Washington waters, especially the Columbia, head northward in their migration, where many are later caught in mixed-stock ocean fisheries
    • This is a prominant reason for the decrease in salmon
