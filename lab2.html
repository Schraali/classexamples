Overfishing

The overharvesting and fishing of Chinook salmon can impact their population and individual sizes dramatically.
This is esepecially evident when faced with these two statistics and the many others like them.

  • according to a 2012 study by the Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife, commercial sport and tribal fishing reduces the amount of four and five-year-old Chinook salmon by about 20%.
  • from 1975 to 2010, almost 20 million Chinook have been harvested for commercial, sport or subsistence and ceremonial fisheries.
